Third Act.

Four more directions. New font — Cinzel. Roman letterforms designed for film contexts. Gold fixed on light backgrounds. Each concept explores a different cinematic grammar.

Font: Cinzel — drawn from Roman inscriptions. Every letterform is geometric, authoritative, designed to be read at distance. Used across film, documentary, and broadcast title design.

E
Leader

A film leader countdown — the circle, the crosshair, the flicker of numbers. Then it resolves into the mark. The in-joke for anyone who's been in a cutting room. The signal that something is about to begin.

Reference: Universal leader. SMPTE bars. BFI archive.

Screen
Print

Notes

  • The countdown flickers at real film speed — roughly one frame per number then holds on the mark.
  • The circle is a leader ring; it could also work as a simple seal badge at static-only scale.
  • Web-only animation — in print the static version shows the resolved GH inside the ring.
  • Cinzel numerals inside the ring are genuinely cinematic — precise Roman-derived letterforms.
F
Stamp

A circular seal. The name runs around the perimeter in small Cinzel capitals. GH draws in the centre. Works as an embossed wax seal, a watermark, or a favicon. Clean authority — the kind of mark that's been on correspondence for a century.

Reference: BFI. The British Film Commission. Screen Ireland.

Screen
Print

Notes

  • The outer ring draws first, inner ring second, perimeter text fades in, then GH draws.
  • Gold on light panel: switched to #7A5C1E dark bronze — same hue, readable on pale backgrounds.
  • The circular form works as a wax seal on print, a favicon at 32px, or a watermark on stills.
  • Perimeter text: adjustable — could be "GREG HOBDEN · FILM PRODUCER · EST. 1994 ·" etc.
G
Wipe

A hard vertical wipe sweeps left to right — exactly like an edit cut. The name is revealed behind it. No morph, no dissolve. A wipe is the oldest editing transition. This is a logo for someone who edits.

Reference: Star Wars wipes. Hitchcock titles. The edit itself.

Screen
Print

Notes

  • A single gold vertical line sweeps across — exactly one edit cut. Everything behind it is revealed.
  • The name is fully formed from frame one — the wipe is the reveal, not a draw or fade.
  • In print: the static version shows the name in full with no bar. Clean wordmark.
  • Cinzel's Roman letterforms give "GREG HOBDEN" the weight a wipe reveal deserves.
H
Title Card

No mark at all. Just the name — in Cinzel, stacked large, with a gold rule between the two lines. Each line arrives from below, one beat apart. This is how films open. The name is the mark. The discipline line sets the register.

Reference: Kubrick intertitles. Lean epics. Saul Bass at scale.

Screen
Print

Notes

  • Cinzel at this scale has genuine authority — the letterforms are derived from Roman stone carvings.
  • No mark needed at display sizes — the name carries everything. GH monogram pairs as a compact variant.
  • Gold rule on light panel is dark bronze #7A5C1E — same hue as the brand gold, readable on pale backgrounds.
  • On print: set at 48pt on A4 white, this is a letterhead. Nothing else on the page.
